# Script Audit Rubric — The Consolidated Audit

**One audit. Sixteen named checks. Every script, every time.**

This rubric replaced the parallel systems (8 Lite gates + 19 failure modes + 12-category Full rubric + 5 source/clarity pillars + 4 Story-Shape checks — ~60 overlapping questions) at v3.0. Every genuinely distinct question those systems asked survives below, exactly once, under a stable **name**. Names, not numbers: checks are cited as `HOOK`, `CITATION`, etc., so future edits never trigger a renumbering cascade. The § Legacy Map at the end translates old Gate/Mode/Pillar/Category references.

Be strict. Do not adjust results to be kind. Real production money depends on this discipline.

## Calibration rule (mandatory, inherited from the Story-Shape recalibration)

**Every check below must PASS all ten anchor scripts in `../00_SHARED_KB/REFERENCE_SCRIPTS_CORE.md` unchanged.** The references are the operating definition of "in tune." A reading of any check that would fail a reference is a miscalibrated reading, not a script defect. Recalibrate the check, never the script.

## Group A — Story & Voice (9 checks, PASS/FAIL + quoted evidence)

### HOOK
The opening 1–5 seconds land a counterintuitive claim, sharp contrast, or concrete comparison the viewer did not expect — the reference hook families. A nod-at-it declarative PASSES (channel signature). Line two proves, sharpens, or opens the causal promise — never abstract restatement.
- FAIL if: greeting / context-first / caveat-first; a bare receipt with no tension when a materially stronger claim, contrast, or carrier was available; the hook claims more than the body's facts support (survivability: would the most-informed viewer's correction become the top comment?); more than one numerical relationship in the first five seconds, or a denominator/reference switch between adjacent hook lines.
- Evidence: quote the hook verbatim (Required Evidence a).

### STRUCTURE
The seven narrative functions land in the strongest topic-native order with a clear turn, and the story keeps moving — a new fact or real contradiction every 3–6 seconds, no flat middle, no overexplaining (no sentence removable without losing meaning).
- For Real Reason / Sharp Contradiction / practical-misconception lanes: the setup builds the viewer's intuitive belief with **≥3 specific reasons** before contradicting it.
- FAIL if: flat zones; front-loaded context dump (>4 paragraphs before the turn); thin surface logic (<3 reasons on the applicable lanes); padding.

### MECHANISM
The script explains **why**, not just what — and the mechanism is made concrete by the first third: a scene, object, comparison, action chain, **or dense named specifics doing real work** (the Ref 2 / Ref 6 / Ref 7 shape). A staged scene is one valid way to pass, not the requirement.
- Distinct mechanisms keep distinct causal roles (e.g. ENABLE → TRIGGER → CLOSE); merged mechanisms ("sunlight causes algae and plastic degradation") FAIL. Explicit First/Second labels optional.
- Human-choice mechanisms (incentive, trust, price, habit) show at least one observable action chain — what the person sees, does, skips, or chooses.
- FAIL if: mechanism stays in abstract nouns throughout; the Stage-1 carrier was dropped for a demonstrably weaker substitute; Wikipedia-summary "what happened" with no why.
- Evidence: name the concreteness carrier — what can the editor show without writing the thesis on screen (Required Evidence d).

### PAYOFF
The close answers the hook's promise with a line the viewer can repeat verbatim. It lands as a sequenced reveal or a single genuinely-true cause — never a flat additive list, and never a single-cause overclaim. Run the **competing-cause test**: if the named mechanism were removed, would other real causes still partly explain the outcome? If yes, "the whole reason / the only reason / that is the whole business model" FAILS — rewrite to the honest both/and or the sequenced roles ("The margin makes popcorn valuable. The counter closes the sale. But the smell starts it.").
- The accurate real rule is stated before the punchline when the catchy answer could leave a new oversimplification.
- The punchline is the **last spoken line** — nothing follows it (run the Ending Subtraction Test: remove each closing line in turn; cut any whose removal lands harder).
- FAIL if: summary instead of answer; motivational/LinkedIn drift; generic CTA; line after the punchline; memorable-but-inaccurate close.

### VOICE
Complete Indian English, spoken not written, restrained not performed — and unmistakably this channel.
- **Article-feel test (most commonly missed — check twice):** join the densest 3 consecutive reveal lines into one paragraph. If it reads like documentary/magazine/Wikipedia prose, FAIL. Also FAIL on 2+-sentence glued paragraph blocks, stacked cognitive anchors in one line, or written connectives ("While X, Y" / "In addition to") that overload first listen — and on the inverse failure: a fragment-poem of dozens of stub lines (the references are paragraph-shaped prose with periodic single-line punches).
- FAIL if: any banned word/opening/tic (per `../00_SHARED_KB/LANGUAGE_AND_VOICE.md`); Hindi/Hinglish; reaction-acting tics; documentary/news/podcast/hype-channel/Wikipedia-compression drift; forced sophistication (unearned essay density — would a sharp friend say this aloud?); host-as-expert framing; energy register mismatched to topic.
- Evidence: 2 spoken-cadence lines quoted + the joined article-feel paragraph + verdict (Required Evidence b, c).

### COMPREHENSION  *(single owner: `../00_SHARED_KB/LANGUAGE_AND_VOICE.md`)*
One check, three layers, all owned by the language file — run them as written there:
1. **Plain Word Test** (word level, § Reader Benchmark): no word a Class 10–12 educated Indian metro reader would look up, unless defined immediately inline. Default one technical term per script.
2. **Instant Comprehension Gate** (phrase level, § The Instant Comprehension Gate): every line lands on first listen — no source-register phrasing, no unexplained institutions, no citation furniture in the spoken track (§ Source-Language vs Viewer-Language).
3. **Mental math / reference stability** (§ Hook Rule + § Sentence Rhythm): ≤1 numerical relationship in the first five seconds; no adjacent denominator/reference switch without an explicit reset; no line that makes the viewer hold one calculation while decoding another; finance terms match what the source measures.
- Evidence: quote any failing line, or record "none" (Required Evidence f).

#### Audience-English Accessibility Gate (runs inside COMPREHENSION — not a 17th check)

A script fails COMPREHENSION if it cannot be understood by a curious Indian Tier 1 / Tier 2 viewer with below-average English comfort (`../00_SHARED_KB/LANGUAGE_AND_VOICE.md § Below-Average English Comprehension Rule`).

Check:

**Vocabulary**

- Does the script use trade jargon?
- Does it use academic or business-heavy language?
- Can every important word be understood instantly?
- Are unavoidable hard names cushioned?

**Sentence Rhythm**

- Are most sentences 8–12 words?
- Are any sentences longer than 15 words (without a factual claim that requires it)?
- Are long sentences broken cleanly?
- Does each sentence carry only one idea?

**Mechanism Clarity**

- Is the mechanism explained in simple cause-effect?
- Are abstract ideas replaced with concrete images?
- Can the viewer follow the story without prior knowledge?

**Production Readability**

- Is the script delivered in beat blocks (one narrative beat per block, 1–4 short sentences per block, blank line between blocks)? One dense paragraph fails; one sentence per block throughout also fails (fragment poem).
- Are hook, reveal, mechanism, scale, and payoff visually separated?
- Can an anchor read each block in one breath?

Fail the script if it sounds like an article, lecture, business case study, or academic explanation. A script delivered as one dense paragraph fails Production Readability even when every line is individually clear.

### PATTERN-FRESHNESS
The channel must not clone itself or others, and must not hammer its thesis.
- **Self-clone:** strip proper nouns; compare the hook/pivot/closer skeleton against the recent rows of `../00_SHARED_KB/SLATE_LEDGER.md`. All three matching a recent Short = FAIL. If the last two Shorts closed on the two-part inversion, this one must close differently. Two consecutive Shorts may not share a mid-script template (the ledger's mid-script-template column: e.g. `both-sides-inside-own-X`, `that-is-the-mechanism`, `validate-then-break`).
- **Repetition:** the one idea is stated once where it lands and once at the close; restatements that add no new causal role, image, or consequence FAIL.
- **External-creator clone:** could this be re-credited to a known channel unnoticed? Must differ in ≥3 of hook / framing / mechanism angle / close / visual treatment. **Saturated topics** (McDonald's-real-estate, Netflix binge-vs-weekly, "Nokia missed the iPhone") must lead with a genuinely lesser-known hook.
- Evidence: skeleton + closer shape vs ledger (Required Evidence e).

### INDIAN-RELEVANCE
Natural or absent — never pasted on, never merely labelled. Where Indian context is present it is a concrete lived condition, familiar contrast, or practical behaviour ("sitting under direct sunlight, every day, for years"), not a label ("on an Indian terrace, this is a problem"). Four of sixteen references carry no Indian framing; absence is a valid PASS. Subtraction test: remove the Indian beat — if the script reads the same or better, the beat was not earned.

### MODEL-INTEGRITY
A Stage-1 mental model, when present, is natural and earned by the mechanism — never invented, forced, or bolted on at audit or rewrite. Absence = `N/A — optional bonus`, never a penalty. A named principle that would fit fewer than 3 other situations, or would fit 4 unrelated topics without changing a word, is fake — FAIL and repair the causal setup or cut the line.

## Group B — Safety (5 checks, binary, ANY FAIL BLOCKS LOCK)

These five are production blockers with no averaging and no "strong enough to ship anyway." A FAIL here = `Status: NEEDS-ANOTHER-PASS` until repaired.

### CITATION  *(single owner: `../00_SHARED_KB/SOURCE_AND_FACT_RULES.md § Citation Integrity` + `§ The Source Dossier`)*
For every load-bearing claim that names or implies a source: outlet/journal exactly right, author and year right, document type right (a review is not a primary study), and the source supports the **exact** claim at the **same scope** (one company ≠ an industry; observed effect ≠ intent). The Source Dossier is filled per claim from the source itself; re-fetch and freshness-check rules per the owner file. No fetch, no lock.

### AUTHORITY  *(owner: `../00_SHARED_KB/LANGUAGE_AND_VOICE.md § Authority Placement`)*
No vague authority ("studies show / researchers found") and no rhetorical authority doing evidentiary work ("every analyst", "everyone knows", "most economists agree"). Speak recognizable or story-central authorities; speak the study shape otherwise; full citation on-screen/in notes. Every appeal to what is known is checkable or rephrased to claim nothing it cannot back.

### CONTESTED-CLAIMS
No genuinely contested academic or historical interpretation stated as plain fact (Sen's entitlement reading, colonial-policy causation, disputed economics). Attribute as a named argument ("X argued, in a reading still debated, that…"). Soft hedges only where the uncertainty can be named; a hedge is never a substitute for a source. Direct viral quotes are NOT softened — the receipt stays visible (Viral lane).

### HARD-NUMBERS
Every specific figure presented as fact (totals, counts, percentages, dates) carries a source + date in the fact-check notes. Unverifiable numbers are softened or cut, never published. Financial terms are precise: revenue / gross margin / operating profit / net profit are not interchangeable, and a margin figure is not a "profit engine" claim without profit-contribution evidence.

### TEMPORAL-FRESHNESS  *(owner: `../00_SHARED_KB/SOURCE_AND_FACT_RULES.md § Temporal Freshness Rule`)*
Every time-relative word ("latest", "currently", "today", "this year", "now") is re-fetched against a primary source on the audit date; exact older periods used as present-day proof are checked against the newest comparable evidence; relative words are replaced with exact periods where they add no value.

## Conditional: Viral Social Commentary Overlay

Run when the script declares Viral Social Commentary OR the topic matches `../00_SHARED_KB/VIRAL_SOCIAL_COMMENTARY.md § When This Lane Applies` even if another lane was declared. The script must quote or clearly show all six:

1. Viral object + price / pay / time / rule in the hook.
2. What the service / behaviour actually does.
3. Side A steelman with a concrete benefit or stakeholder.
4. Side B steelman with wage / price / behaviour math.
5. Direct viral evidence when the original wording is the proof.
6. Discomfort + the three-beat close (compressed insight, stakes-naming viewer question, named-audience share).

**Beat-5 wording check:** the granting-both-sides line must be topic-native. "Both sides are right — inside their own X" with a swapped noun clones References 15/16 — FAIL (counts against PATTERN-FRESHNESS) and rewrite the line. The beat is mandatory; the reference wording is not.

Any missing item = lane drift, Rewrite Trigger = Yes. **Op-Ed Aphorism Test:** read the closing 2 lines aloud; a magazine-kicker aphorism close ("India did not invent the helper; it priced the helper") is an auto-fail — the closer in this lane is the viewer question. A clean observation without the debate payload is the failure, no matter how polished.

## Required Evidence (a)–(f) — un-evidenced passes are auto-fail

A PASS without its evidence quoted inline auto-converts to FAIL. Paste all six blocks in every audit:

- **(a) Hook quoted** — verbatim single line or 2–3 line stack; confirm no over-claim.
- **(b) Spoken-cadence lines** — 2 verbatim lines, clearly spoken not written-formal; no reaction tics, no Hinglish.
- **(c) Article-feel test** — the densest 3 consecutive reveal lines, joined as a paragraph, + verdict `Spoken-feel / Article-feel`.
- **(d) Concreteness carrier** — the scene/object/comparison/action-chain/named-specifics that carries the mechanism + what the editor can show without writing the thesis on screen.
- **(e) Self-clone** — proper-nouns-stripped skeleton + closer shape + mid-script template vs the last SLATE_LEDGER rows + verdict.
- **(f) Comprehension** — any line failing word-level, phrase-level, or mental-math clarity, quoted; or "none."

## Verdict Labels (derived from the checks — use exactly these)

| Verdict | Condition | Rewrite Trigger |
|---|---|---|
| **Strong** | 16/16 PASS (+ overlay if applicable). | No — Direct finalization. |
| **Good but needs polish** | Group B all PASS; ≤2 Group A FAILs, each repairable by line-level fixes without restructuring a beat (the Fix List fully covers them). | No — fixes applied at finalization. |
| **Generic** | Any FAIL on HOOK, STRUCTURE, MECHANISM, PAYOFF, or PATTERN-FRESHNESS; or >2 Group A FAILs. | Yes — Major rewrite. |
| **Off-brand** | VOICE FAIL (identity/category drift), regardless of other results. | Yes. |
| **Fact-risky** | Any Group B FAIL. | Yes if the claim cannot survive as written; otherwise No and finalization repairs (verify / soften / cut). |
| **Reject** | Pervasive failure, or the idea itself fails the Final Disposition re-score below. | Yes — AI reframe if defensible; drop only when no clean reframe exists. |

A failed Viral overlay = Rewrite Trigger Yes (lane rewrite), verdict at most Generic. A factually clean script that fails VOICE or MECHANISM is **not Strong** — the fix is a voice/story rewrite, not a fact rewrite. Safety and story now block with the same force.

## Final Disposition — PASS / FIX / DROP (mandatory, runs last)

Verdicts grade **execution**; this gate grades the **idea** — a clean rewrite must never carry a weak idea to PASS.

**Idea-Strength re-score** (keep identity-bearing proper nouns when the person/place/company/object IS the novelty; strip them only for the separate self-clone skeleton check):

1. **Curiosity gap** — would a viewer actually Google this?
2. **Repeatable payoff** — one line they would send a friend?
3. **Surprise / novelty** — or a saturated explainer they have already seen?
4. **Mechanism strength** — a real hidden reason, not just a fact?
5. **Channel-identity fit** — sharp, insight-led; not a generic explainer?

**Hard rule:** fails ≥2 of five → **DROP**, even at 16/16 execution. Rewrite is only for execution defects on a strong idea. Log the DROP reason in one line. The pipeline does not loop a DROP into rewrites; return to Stage 1 only if a genuinely different angle exists.

- **PASS** — strong idea AND clean execution → production.
- **FIX** — real potential, flawed execution → the Rewrite-Trigger path.
- **DROP** — not worth producing → stop, one-line reason.

## Calibration Anchors

### Hook
- **PASS (stack):** "The hottest thing humans ever made... was not a nuclear bomb. / It was not even the Sun. / It happened inside a 27-kilometre tunnel under Europe." — kills two obvious answers, withholds the mechanism.
- **PASS (single-line — often the more premium choice):** "Britain's most decorated female spy in the Second World War was an Indian princess." — declarative, specific, instantly overturns an assumption.
- **Borderline:** "Global supply chains have a priority list." — conceptual; needs line two immediately.
- **FAIL:** "Did you know Nokia used to be huge?" — banned opening, no specificity.

### Payoff
- **PASS:** "Nokia didn't lose to the iPhone. Nokia lost to Nokia." / "This was never a Diet Coke shortage. India is just lower on the list."
- **PASS (Viral lane):** "Tell me, would you use this service for yourself or for your parents? Share this with somebody who needs to see this." — names the stakeholder, answerable, named-audience share.
- **Borderline:** "So that's how the supply chain works." — summary, not payoff.
- **FAIL:** "What do you think? Let me know in the comments." — generic CTA.

### Mental model
- **Strong:** "History remembers people who fit narratives." / "Constraint produces precision."
- **Fake:** any line that fits four unrelated topics without changing a word.

## Required Audit Output Format

Use the Section I template in `01_PROMPT.md` (this folder). At minimum every audit records: verdict + Rewrite Trigger + Final Disposition; the 16-check table (PASS/FAIL + one-line evidence each); Required Evidence (a)–(f) in full; overlay result when applicable; lane/register/chain checks; What Works / What Feels Weak (specific quotes); the line-level Fix List; Fact-Check Risks; Sources Consulted; counter-argument.
