**Locked spoken script (verbatim):**

> [direct]
> Popcorn's smell has a formula.
> 
> It is not the popcorn itself — it is the chemistry that makes you hungry.
> 
> [beat]
> 
> When popcorn pops, it releases compounds — the ones that give you buttery, roasted, and savory sensations. None of these are the taste of the popcorn. They are purely smell.
> 
> Your nose detects these compounds faster than your taste buds ever could.
> 
> [beat]
> 
> Why? The reason is anatomy. Your smell system connects directly to your brain's hunger center. It signals appetite in milliseconds. Your taste buds, by contrast, travel a slower pathway. So when you smell cinema popcorn, your brain has already decided "I want to eat" before your mouth tastes a single kernel.
> 
> Taste does not decide the hunger. Taste confirms it.
> 
> [beat]
> 
> Cinemas engineered this. The aroma is controlled — when it hits, how strong, how often it reaches you. Because they are not selling popcorn. They are selling the moment your nose hijacks your appetite before your mind intervenes.
> 
> [beat]
> [drop voice]
> 
> The smell hijacks appetite. The taste is just confirmation.
> 
> Your nose made the sale. Your mouth closed the deal.
