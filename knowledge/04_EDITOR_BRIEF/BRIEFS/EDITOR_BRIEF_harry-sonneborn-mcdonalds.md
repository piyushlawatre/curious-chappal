## 1. Script (locked — do not change a word)

- **Title:** The Man Who Actually Built McDonald's Was Not Ray Kroc
- **Thumbnail text:** NOT RAY KROC
- **Hook stack:** "In 1954, Ray Kroc was nearly broke. / He thought he was in the burger business. / A man named Harry Sonneborn told him he had it completely wrong."
- **Payoff:** "McDonald's does not sell burgers. It rents land to the person who does."
- **Host tone:** Energy 7/10, Pace measured — slow on the Sonneborn-insight beat and payoff line, Warmth restrained with dry-irony on the closer
- **Strong video title:** The Man Who Actually Built McDonald's Was Not Ray Kroc
- **Lane signature label:** Used — **SMART MONEY** chip, small, at the Franchise Realty Corporation reveal beat; see Section 4

**Locked spoken script (verbatim):**

> [direct]
> In 1954, Ray Kroc was nearly broke.
> He was collecting a small cut — roughly 1.4 percent — of every burger a franchisee sold.
> The franchisees were making money. Kroc was not.
>
> [beat]
> That year, a finance executive named Harry Sonneborn walked in.
> He had been working at a fast-food chain called Tastee-Freez.
> He looked at Kroc's numbers and said: you are thinking about this wrong.
>
> [pause]
> Stop collecting a cut of the burgers.
> [stress] Buy the land under the restaurant.
> Then lease that land to the franchisee.
> You become the landlord.
> They pay you rent every month, whether the burgers sell or not.
>
> [beat]
> Sonneborn set up a company inside McDonald's called the Franchise Realty Corporation.
> McDonald's would buy or lease plots of land, then rent them back to franchisees at a higher price.
> If a franchisee broke the rules, McDonald's could evict them.
> The land gave McDonald's control the burger never could.
>
> [pause]
> By 1960, McDonald's had opened close to two hundred locations.
> Not because it had a better burger.
> Because it owned the ground those restaurants were standing on.
>
> [beat]
> In 2025, McDonald's earned sixteen and a half billion dollars from its franchise operation.
> Ninety-five percent of its restaurants are franchised.
> McDonald's does not run most of those kitchens.
> It collects rent on the land beneath them.
>
> [slow]
> McDonald's does not sell burgers. It rents land to the person who does.
>
> [drop voice]
> That idea came from one man, whose name almost nobody knows.
