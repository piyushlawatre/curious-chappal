# Thumbnail Design SOP

Canonical source for Curious Chappal thumbnail dimensions, palette, typography, composition, and banned thumbnail elements.

## Thumbnail Purpose

The thumbnail's job is to communicate the Short's hook and curiosity gap in under 2 seconds. It should make the viewer want to stop scrolling — without resorting to hype, reaction faces, or clickbait.

The thumbnail is not entertainment bait. It is the visual version of our hook or hook stack.

## Aspect Ratio and Dimensions

- **Shorts / Reels thumbnails:** 1080×1920 pixels (9:16)
- **Long-form thumbnails:** 1280×720 pixels (16:9)
- **Resolution:** Minimum 72 DPI, target 144 DPI

## Color Palette

Strictly three colors:

- **Deep navy** (#162447) — primary background or dominant element
- **Mustard yellow** (#E8B04B) — accent color
- **White** (#FFFFFF) — secondary text and clean elements

A fourth color, signal red (#E63946), may be used only for current-affairs or "scam reveal" thumbnails where urgency or alert is essential. Maximum one in five thumbnails may use red.

No gradients. No multi-color schemes. No saturation experiments.

## Typography

- **Primary impact text:** Poppins ExtraBold, 90–200pt depending on word count
- **Supporting text:** Inter Bold, 45–65pt
- Maximum two type sizes per thumbnail
- No decorative or script fonts
- No outline-only text
- No 3D extruded text
- No glow effects beyond a subtle 2pt black stroke for legibility

## Composition Rules

### Layout Type 1: Text-Dominant (analytical or observational Shorts)

- Large impact text occupies 60–70% of the frame
- Navy background, white text with one mustard word for emphasis
- No imagery, or minimal supporting graphic
- Used for: Nokia, Diet Coke, South Korea, ISRO, bamboo law, supply-chain Shorts

### Layout Type 2: Subject + Text (human / personality / history)

- Single subject occupies 40–50% of frame
- Text occupies remaining frame, no overlap
- Subject can be photograph or illustration
- Used for: Noor Inayat Khan, Trump / Mar-a-Lago, host-led topics

### Layout Type 3: Number-Dominant (data reveal / cost / scale)

- Single number occupies 50% of frame
- Supporting context in smaller text
- Used for: ISRO cost comparison, 45-trillion-dollar bamboo Short, fertility-rate Short

## Text Rules

- Maximum 5 words on any thumbnail
- All caps for impact text only
- One sentence / claim per thumbnail
- The text must complete or amplify the video title, not duplicate it
- The text should hint at the hook OR at the payoff — not both

## Subject Treatment

- **Faces:** Color-graded consistently — slight desaturation, warm temperature shift
- **Historical photos:** Maintain original character
- **Objects:** Clean background, isolated subject, soft shadow
- **No reaction faces:** Shocked expressions, open mouths, pointing fingers banned
- **No exaggerated expressions:** The face conveys gravity or curiosity, not surprise

## Visual Elements That Are Banned

- Emoji of any kind
- Red circles drawn around elements
- Arrows pointing at things
- Hype-style "VS" brackets, hard split screens, or adversarial comparison graphics. A clean side-by-side comparison is allowed when the comparison itself is the story carrier.
- Photoshop manipulation that looks editorial
- Lens flares, particle effects, sparkles
- Speed lines or motion blur
- Stock illustration mascots
- Multi-photo collages
- Borders or frames around the thumbnail
- Channel logo or watermark on the thumbnail

## Banned Words on Thumbnails

Canonical source: `../00_SHARED_KB/LANGUAGE_AND_VOICE.md`. Qualifiers matter — `CRAZY` is banned as standalone hype, not as a literal descriptor inside a sourced claim.

- SHOCKING
- INSANE
- CRAZY (as hype)
- MIND-BLOWING
- YOU WON'T BELIEVE
- UNBELIEVABLE
- BOMBSHELL
- EXPOSED
- THE TRUTH ABOUT
- SECRET
- FORBIDDEN
- WORST/BEST EVER
- ULTIMATE
- DESTROYED

## Approved Thumbnail Text Patterns

- **The declarative claim:** "INDIA WAS LAST" / "NOKIA LOST TO NOKIA"
- **The specific number:** "MARS FOR LESS" / "A HUNDRED TIMES" / "45 TRILLION LOST"
- **The named pattern (Anchor / Mental Model / Framework):** "EXPORT OR DIE" / "INCUMBENT CAPTURE"
- **The identity reveal:** "THE SPY BRITAIN FORGOT"
- **The mechanism name:** "YOUR BODY ALREADY DOES THIS"
- **The understated truth:** "THE DRIP YOU DON'T NEED"
- **The hidden reason:** "THE LAW STILL COSTING INDIA"
- **The sharp contradiction:** "INDIA BANNED IT. AUSTRALIA LEGALISED IT."
- **The Indian observation:** "MADE ALMOST RIGHT"

## Anchor Thumbnail Notes

Thumbnails can lean toward concrete claims, surprising numbers, recognisable Indian observations, or the mental model itself when the model is the line everyone will repeat ("NOKIA LOST TO NOKIA", "EXPORT OR DIE").

All Anchor thumbnails use the same color palette, typefaces, and composition rules. The differentiation is in the text choice, not the design system.

## Recurring Format Thumbnail Notes

Format lanes from `../00_SHARED_KB/FORMAT_LANES.md` should influence thumbnail language, not create new design systems.

- **Real Reason:** text hints at the hidden cause ("NOT THE IPHONE", "THE REAL REASON")
- **Hidden India:** text uses Indian specificity ("INDIA WAS LAST", "THE DRIP YOU DON'T NEED")
- **Smart Money/Business:** text names the money mechanism or decision ("NOKIA LOST TO NOKIA", "SUPPLY CHAIN PLAY")
- **Science Lite:** text names the invisible mechanism simply ("YOUR BODY ALREADY DOES THIS")
- **Sharp Contradiction:** text puts the contradiction on the surface ("BANNED HERE. LEGAL THERE.")

Do not add format labels to every thumbnail. The format should be felt through the claim.

## Approval Checklist

Before publishing any thumbnail:

- [ ] Maximum 5 words of text
- [ ] Only navy, mustard, and white (red allowed once per five for current affairs / scam reveals)
- [ ] Only Poppins ExtraBold and Inter Bold typefaces
- [ ] No emoji, arrows, circles, or reaction faces
- [ ] No banned words
- [ ] Text completes or amplifies the title
- [ ] Thumbnail claim matches the selected recurring format lane
- [ ] Single subject if photo is used
- [ ] Composition tested at small size
- [ ] Thumbnail signals smart short-form, not hype and not academic
- [ ] Brand-safe (no partisan campaign-style or inflammatory political/religious imagery; necessary factual people, institutions, or symbols may appear with neutral restraint)

If any box is unchecked, redesign before publishing.

## Thumbnail Aesthetic Principles

Use clean text on flat colour, one sharp claim in 3–5 words, large legible type at small sizes, one subject if a photo is used, no reaction faces, no arrows/circles/multi-colour. Calibrate against our internal approved layouts (Text-Dominant / Subject+Text / Number-Dominant), not against external creators.

Do not imitate hype-channel thumbnails (rainbow text, reaction faces, red circles, vs-style splits).
