# Master Rule — Curious Chappal Knowledge Base

**Read this before opening any other KB file.**

**Master KB version: v3.9.** This is the single version stamp for the whole Knowledge Base. Every stage stamps this value on its output (the `KB version:` line). When the KB changes materially, bump it here — not per-stage — so that two outputs are comparable when and only when they share this number.

This file states, in one place, how the Knowledge Base works together. Paste it into any AI session that needs to operate the pipeline correctly.

## Reference Hierarchy

### 1. REFERENCE_SCRIPTS.md — Quality Source of Truth

The sixteen complete Anchor Short scripts in `REFERENCE_SCRIPTS.md` are the creative quality benchmark for this channel.

If a creative-calibration question conflicts with a summary, the references win on voice, pacing, hook craft, mechanism reveal, and closing feel. They do **not** override newer operational, safety, sourcing, factual-integrity, stage-ownership, or output-schema rules. Those are controlled by the owning canonical file.

**Use the references for:** voice calibration, hook craft, mechanism reveal, mental-model placement, pacing, closing discipline, factual specificity.

**Do not use the references for:** topic templates, factual reuse, or hook copying. They are calibration material, not source material.

### 2. Supporting Operating Files

Every other file in this folder exists to operationalise what the references show:

- `CHANNEL_CONSTITUTION.md` — channel identity, audience, what the channel is and is not.
- `CONTEXT_PRIMER.md` — compressed brief to paste into a new AI chat.
- `LANGUAGE_AND_VOICE.md` — the complete Indian English register and how the host sounds. **The authoritative file on language. Read this before any script work.**
- `FORMAT_LANES.md` — the seven canonical lanes plus three proposed lanes, with lane-selection rules.
- `VIRAL_SOCIAL_COMMENTARY.md` — full spec for the Viral Social Commentary lane (the one lane that overrides the default mechanism-reveal structure).
- `SOURCE_AND_FACT_RULES.md` — sourcing, fact-risk grades, and the verification discipline that produces the kind of specificity visible in the references.
- `SOURCE_INTEGRITY_AND_CLARITY_GATE.md` — mandatory five-pillar final lock gate for source integrity, mechanism honesty, authority placement, first-listen clarity, and temporal freshness.

If supporting files disagree, use the owning canonical file for the decision type. Use the references only as the creative-calibration tie-breaker.

### 3. Deprecated

There is no "Standard Short" production target. There is no second tier of Shorts. Every produced Short is an Anchor Short. Any file or note that points to a "Standard Short" benchmark is outdated and must be corrected.

## Core Production Rules (Non-Negotiable)

**Every produced Short is an Anchor Short.** 230 to 280 spoken words, 90 to 120 seconds, written for an educated urban Indian viewer aged eighteen to forty in a Tier 1 or Tier 2 city, comfortable in English, watching during a commute or break. This is the hard production envelope. If the idea only works materially shorter or longer, reframe it or move it to another format; do not pad or over-compress it.

**The language register is complete Indian English.** The channel is not a Hinglish fact channel. The register is complete Indian English — globally understandable English shaped by Indian context. Hindi or Hinglish as the primary register, internet-speak, "bro / guys / bhai", emojis, and exclamation marks are out. Contractions are rare. Hinglish does not appear in any of the sixteen reference scripts. See `LANGUAGE_AND_VOICE.md` for the full register — that file is the authoritative language guide.

**The script must perform seven narrative functions, not obey one rigid beat order.** A strong Short creates a hook, supplies enough context, turns the viewer's understanding, explains the real mechanism, uses Indian relevance only when natural, delivers the earned insight, and lands a closing line. These functions may interleave or recur when the topic's strongest story spine requires it. A flat conventional-explanation pivot or separate mental-model line is not mandatory when the turn and insight are already clear. Viral Social Commentary still uses its lane-specific debate structure.

**Mental model — not a hard gate, never forced, but its absence is a diagnostic.** A transferable mental model is never a scoring gate and must never be invented, faked, or bolted on. Absence alone cannot lower a score or verdict. It may prompt the evaluator to re-examine the already-scored mechanism, novelty, and payoff gates; only weakness in those gates changes the verdict. The Viral Social Commentary lane uses a compressed-insight + viewer-question + named-audience-share close instead.

**Factual claims are checkable.** Numbers come with units. Vague authority is out. Speak recognizable or story-central authorities; when unfamiliar names add no viewer value, speak the study shape and keep the full source on-screen/in notes.

**Accuracy repairs must preserve story force.** Verification can soften, replace, or cut unsafe claims; it cannot automatically replace a strong scene, conflict, character, or image with accounting ratios or source exposition. Every major rewrite identifies the original's strongest narrative asset, searches for source-backed story evidence, and proves the final is at least as watchable on hook pull, concrete imagery, cognitive load, and payoff.

**A narrative asset is not enough; protect the story spine.** Every topic handoff names the narrative proposition, strongest source-backed asset, intended story spine, and strongest safe keeper line.

**A sourced proposition is not a concrete story carrier.** Every topic handoff also names the pictureable scene, object, comparison, or action chain that lets the viewer see the mechanism. The carrier may be source-backed or a clearly illustrative viewer-world scene that invents no specific event, motive, quote, company action, or measured result. If an editor cannot storyboard it without writing the thesis itself on screen, it fails the Pictureability Test.

**Human behaviour must be staged, not merely labelled.** When a mechanism concerns choice, incentives, trust, pricing, or habit, the script includes at least one observable action chain showing what the person sees, does, skips, or chooses.

**Passing is not the same as optimised.** Before final lock, compare at least two viable full treatments when a materially different safe treatment exists. Choose the strongest on hook pull, causal clarity, cognitive load, payoff, and ending force.

**First-listen clarity includes mental work, not only vocabulary.** A line fails when the words are plain but the viewer must switch denominators, track changing reference groups, retain multiple ratios, or decode citation details at scroll speed. The first five seconds carry no more than one numerical relationship.

**Current-function facts are rechecked at final lock.** Time-relative words require a fresh primary-source check. Exact older periods also require a freshness check when they function as proof of the present.

**Every Short declares a format lane before scripting.** Seven canonical lanes (Real Reason, Hidden India, Smart Money/Business, Science Lite, Sharp Contradiction, Viral Social Commentary, One-off) plus three proposed lanes calibrated in `REFERENCE_SCRIPTS.md` (Forgotten Inventor, Quiet Monopoly, Status Game). One-off requires a written justification in the brief.

## Stage Ownership

Each stage owns one job. The owning canonical file is authoritative for that decision. Prompts, READMEs, primers, and reference summaries may repeat a rule for convenience, but they are subordinate and cannot override the owner.

| Stage | Owns |
|-------|------|
| 01_TOPIC_EVALUATION | Is this worth making? What is the format lane? What is the hook direction? |
| 02_SCRIPT_CREATION | Write the script. Self-audit against the seven narrative functions, strongest topic-native order, and the language register. |
| 03_AUDIT_AND_FINALIZE | Audit the draft against the reference benchmark (locked first), then apply the fix list, verify all load-bearing facts, and lock the final script. Merges the former standalone audit + rewrite stages. |
| 04_EDITOR_BRIEF | Translate the locked script into visual / editing guidance only. No script edits. |

## What Must Never Happen

- A Short written in Hindi or Hinglish as its primary register. The channel's default register is complete Indian English. Hinglish does not appear in any of the sixteen reference scripts.
- A Short that drifts into documentary-explainer, magazine-article, or academic register.
- A mental model invented because the script needed one.
- A factual claim stated with certainty when the source does not support that certainty.
- A reference to "Standard Short" as a calibration target.
- A reference to AevyTV, Vox, Wendover, Kurzgesagt, or any external creator as a style target or source of truth.
- A script outside 230–280 words or 90–120 seconds.
- A closing line that is a motivational quote, a LinkedIn aphorism, or a generic CTA.

## Quick Calibration Test (Before Recording)

Six questions for any final script:

1. Does the opening land a counterintuitive claim, sharp contrast, or pictureable comparison, with no greeting and no "imagine if"?
2. Is there a clear pivot away from the conventional explanation, followed by a mechanism that earns the new reading?
3. If Stage 1 supplied a mental model, does it arrive late and land in one or two sentences (or, in Viral Social Commentary, does the required three-beat close land)?
4. Is the language complete Indian English — no internet-speak, no "bro / guys / bhai", no emojis, no exclamation marks, no Hindi or Hinglish?
5. Can every load-bearing fact be traced to a checkable source, with time-relative claims and present-function evidence freshly rechecked?
6. If this was a major rewrite, is it demonstrably at least as watchable as the draft it replaced?

If yes on all six, the script is ready. If no on any, fix the failure before recording.
