# Minimum Context for New AI

This primer is a subordinate summary. Canonical rules live in the owning files (pointers below); if anything here conflicts with them, the owner wins. The creative benchmark is always `REFERENCE_SCRIPTS_CORE.md` / `REFERENCE_SCRIPTS.md`.

## Channel Identity

A short-form video channel (YouTube Shorts / Instagram Reels) that takes interesting stories — surprising facts, hidden mechanisms, smart observations — and packages them sharply enough that the viewer feels a little smarter within 60–120 seconds. Mechanism-first: the channel exists to explain the part of a familiar story the viewer did not know.

It sits between loud Hinglish fact-channels and dry long-form documentary explainers. The differentiation is *register*, not vocabulary mixing: complete Indian English, restrained voice, named specifics ("Rockefeller University's biology lab", "450 crore rupees", "Mangalyaan") doing the work hype words attempt elsewhere.

NOT: a documentary-explainer in Short form, a magazine article on video, a podcast clip, a "did you know" Hinglish fact channel, a news/lecture/lifestyle/comedy/party-political channel.

## Audience

Educated urban Indian viewers, 18–40, Tier 1/Tier 2 cities, watching during a commute or break — and possibly with below-average English comfort. Vocabulary ceiling: a **Class 10–12 educated reader** — unfamiliar terms are explained immediately or replaced. They recognise lakh, crore, Mangalyaan, dengue without translation. They will not tolerate greetings, "guys/bro/bhai", emojis, filler, fake-hype, or motivational closes.

## Language

**Complete Indian English.** No Hindi or Hinglish, no internet-speak, no emojis, no exclamation marks; contractions rare. If a line feels weak and the fix that comes to mind is a Hindi word, the line is weak — fix the English. Full register, banned lists, hook shapes, and comprehension rules: `LANGUAGE_AND_VOICE.md` (the authoritative language file).

## Production Envelope

Every produced Short is an **Anchor Short**: 200–280 spoken words, 60–120 seconds. Hard envelope — reframe or change formats rather than pad or over-compress.

## Structure

Seven narrative functions, order flexible to the topic's strongest story spine: hook (counterintuitive claim/contrast, no greeting) → compressed context with named specifics → pivot away from the conventional explanation → real mechanism (the hidden why) → Indian relevance only when earned → mental model only when natural (never forced; absence is fine) → one closing line that compresses the insight, then stop.

**Lane exception:** Viral Social Commentary uses its own six-beat debate structure and three-beat close (compressed insight + stakes-naming viewer question + named-audience share). See `VIRAL_SOCIAL_COMMENTARY.md`.

## Format Lanes

Declared before scripting; the lane is decided by what the **hook** makes surprising. Seven canonical: Real Reason, Hidden India, Smart Money/Business, Science Lite, Sharp Contradiction, Viral Social Commentary, One-off (requires written justification). Three proposed: Forgotten Inventor, Quiet Monopoly, Status Game. Definitions and tiebreakers: `FORMAT_LANES.md`.
