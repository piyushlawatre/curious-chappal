# Channel Constitution

Canonical source for channel identity, audience, what the channel is, and what it is not. The reference scripts calibrate creative feel; this file remains authoritative for identity decisions.

---

## Channel Identity

A short-form video channel that takes interesting stories — surprising facts, hidden mechanisms, smart observations, useful insights — and packages them clearly enough that the viewer feels a little smarter after sixty to a hundred and twenty seconds.

The target category is **fast, mechanism-first, urban Indian short-form storytelling**. Sharp opening, compressed context with named specifics, a concrete carrier that lets the viewer see the mechanism, a clean pivot, an earned insight, and a clean close. Built native for YouTube Shorts and Instagram Reels.

This is **smart short-form**. It is not a documentary channel, not a magazine article on video, not a compressed mini-documentary, not a podcast clip pretending to be a Short.

---

## One-Line Positioning

Mechanism-first Indian short-form storytelling. Curious stories, told in complete Indian English, in 60 to 120 seconds, with a payoff that holds after the scroll.

---

## Target Audience

**Primary.** Educated urban Indian viewers, aged eighteen to forty. May have below-average English comfort — every script must stay clear on first listen at voiceover speed (see `LANGUAGE_AND_VOICE.md § Below-Average English Comprehension Rule`). Live in Tier 1 metros (Mumbai, Delhi, Bengaluru, Hyderabad, Chennai, Kolkata) and Tier 2 cities (Pune, Indore, Jaipur, Coimbatore, Lucknow, Chandigarh, Ahmedabad, and similar). Scroll Shorts and Reels daily. Curious about money, science, history, business, current debates, and the *real reason behind things*.

**Secondary.** South Asian diaspora viewers globally with the same psychographic.

---

## Audience Psychology

This viewer:

- Will absorb a named specific ("Rockefeller University's biology lab", "Mangalyaan", "WHO treatment guidelines") on first listen when unfamiliar terms are explained immediately.
- Wants the mechanism, not the headline. They already know the surface story; the channel exists to explain the part they did not know.
- Hates "did you know" energy and fake-hype vocabulary.
- Will stop scrolling for specificity (numbers, named institutions, dated events) and share a Short if the closing line is sharp enough to repeat to a friend.
- Switches off when content becomes academic, slow, or padded with filler.
- Appreciates a transferable mental model when it sits at the end of an earned mechanism. A topic that produces no transferable insight is almost always pointing at a weak mechanism. Fix the mechanism, not the beat.

## What the Viewer Should Feel After Watching

- "I knew about this, but I did not know why it actually worked that way."
- "I can see the larger pattern now."
- "I am going to send this to my friend who keeps arguing about this."
- "That was a hundred seconds, and I learned something I can repeat."

The viewer should not feel lectured. The viewer should not feel tricked. The viewer should feel that a familiar topic suddenly became clearer, stranger, or more important than they expected.

---

## What Makes This Channel Different

Most generic Hinglish fact channels are loud, sensational, and shallow on sourcing. Most premium English explainers are too long, too dry, and built for desktop viewing.

This channel sits in between — and that gap is the channel:

- **Smarter than** the generic Hinglish fact-channel category.
- **Lighter and faster than** long-form documentary explainers.
- **Calibrated against** the sixteen Anchor Short references in `REFERENCE_SCRIPTS.md`, not against any external creator's house style.
- **Built native** for short-form, not compressed from long-form.

The differentiation is *register*: complete Indian English, restrained voice, mechanism-first storytelling. Vocabulary mixing is not the differentiator. Specificity is.

## The Lens

Every Short reveals something the viewer did not know — or did not know the *real reason* behind. The story is the product. A transferable mental model appears in fifteen of sixteen references, but it is never a required beat — it lands only when the mechanism earns it (see `MASTER_RULE.md`). The exception is Viral Social Commentary, which swaps the model for a compressed-insight + viewer-question close.

Acceptable Short angles (each backed by a reference script):

- A counterintuitive identity (Ref 1: Britain's most decorated WWII spy was an Indian princess).
- An invisible biological mechanism (Ref 2: mosquitoes hunt specific people; Ref 8: Ozempic is a hormone your body already produces).
- A hidden Indian reason (Ref 3: doctors prescribe drips that do almost nothing; Ref 5: ISRO's Mars mission for less than Hollywood spent on Gravity).
- A business or money mechanism (Ref 4: Nokia lost to itself; Ref 6: South Korea's export-or-die condition).
- A sharp contradiction with a hidden resolution (Ref 9: Kerala's literacy and unemployment paradox).
- A power-structure reveal (Ref 7: supply chains are not logistics).
- A One-off curiosity that earns the slot (Ref 10: the Voyager Golden Record).
- A credit-erasure story (Ref 11: the woman who invented the dishwasher).
- A quiet-monopoly reveal (Ref 12: four firms decide what most of the world eats; Ref 13: De Beers).
- A status-game mechanism (Ref 14: Indian wedding budgets rising while incomes flatten).
- A live Indian debate with a structural shift beneath it (Ref 15: 70-hour week; Ref 16: no kids / one kid).

If a topic fits at least one of these patterns and passes the eight-point filter below, it is approved.

After approval, every Short is assigned a format lane from `FORMAT_LANES.md`. The publishing rule is simple: **every produced Short is an Anchor Short**. Anchor Short is the production target, not a format lane.

## What This Channel Is NOT

- NOT a long-form documentary-explainer in Short form.
- NOT a polished motion-graphic mini-documentary.
- NOT a magazine article squeezed into ninety seconds.
- NOT a high-quality podcast clip presented as a Short.
- NOT a generic "did you know" Hinglish fact channel.
- NOT a news-reaction channel.
- NOT a lecture channel.
- NOT a lifestyle channel.
- NOT a comedy channel.
- NOT a party-political opinion channel. (Socio-economic debate inside the Viral Social Commentary lane is in scope; party / electoral / religious-community side-taking is out of scope.)
- NOT a daily vlog.

---

## What Must Never Happen

- A Short written in Hindi, Hinglish, slang, or internet-speak. (Indian terms of art — lakh, crore, raga — appear naturally without translation, but the language register is complete Indian English.)
- A Short that drifts into documentary-explainer, magazine-article, or academic register.
- A mental model invented because the script needed one.
- "Did you know" framing, channel intros, greetings, or hype-vocabulary openings.
- Sensationalist words. (See `LANGUAGE_AND_VOICE.md` for the banned list.)
- Channel catchphrases spoken inside videos.
- Mocking any community, religion, country, region, profession, or class.
- Party-political side-taking.
- Generic CTAs (like and subscribe; comment your thoughts; tag your friend).
- Motivational drift in the closing line.
- Host-as-expert framing ("I have studied this for years").
- Wikipedia summaries dressed up in our voice.

## Identity Lens (Non-Scoring)

Use these questions to understand channel fit. They are diagnostic, not a second verdict gate. The scored 8-Point Gate and all verdict math live only in `../01_TOPIC_EVALUATION/04_TOPIC_VALIDATION_GUIDE.md` and `../01_TOPIC_EVALUATION/01_PROMPT.md`.

For every Short, ask:

1. Is there a counterintuitive claim, sharp contrast, or pictureable comparison available in the opening?
2. Does the topic have a hidden mechanism — incentive, biology, structure, power dynamic — that the surface story does not reveal?
3. Is there a clear payoff the viewer can repeat to a friend?
4. Is the topic either surprising in itself or surprising in its mechanism?
5. Can the script be voiced in sixty to a hundred and twenty seconds without padding or compression?
6. Will the viewer feel smarter, not lectured, after watching?
7. Does the topic earn complete Indian English without needing Hindi or slang to land?
8. Is there a strong narrative proposition, story spine, and pictureable scene/object/comparison/action chain that can carry the viewer from hook to payoff without padding?

Passing this lens does not itself approve a topic; Stage 1 still applies the scored gate, fact-risk, clone-risk, and auto-downgrade rules.

A transferable mental model is never a hard gate and is never forced. Its absence alone cannot reduce a score or verdict; it may only prompt a closer look at the already-scored mechanism, novelty, and payoff gates. The Viral Social Commentary lane uses its three-beat close instead.
