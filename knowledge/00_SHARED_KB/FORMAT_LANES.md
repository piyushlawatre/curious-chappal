# Format Lanes

Every approved Short declares a format lane before scripting. The lane gives the channel repeatability — a recognisable show instead of a pile of one-offs.

The seven canonical lanes and three proposed lanes below are calibrated against the sixteen scripts in `REFERENCE_SCRIPTS.md`. Each lane lists the reference scripts that calibrate it. Study those references before drafting in that lane.

---

## How To Use This File

1. After topic approval, pick the lane that fits the **hook**, not the topic. (Most strong topics fit two or three lanes; the hook tells you which one.)
2. Open the reference scripts cited for that lane in `REFERENCE_SCRIPTS.md` and read them through.
3. Draft the script so it performs the seven narrative functions (Hook, Context, Turn, Mechanism, optional Indian Relevance, Insight, Close) in the strongest topic-native story spine. Viral Social Commentary uses its own structure.
4. Run the lane's failure-mode list before approving.

If a topic genuinely cannot be assigned to one lane, run it as a **One-off** with a written justification in the brief.

## The Seven Canonical Lanes

| Lane | Use when | Reference scripts |
|------|----------|-------------------|
| Real Reason | The viewer knows the surface story but not the actual cause | Diet Coke Shortage (Ref 7) |
| Hidden India | Indian-ness *is* the surprise — the topic is rooted in Indian lived experience, history, infrastructure, business, or behaviour | Noor Inayat Khan (Ref 1), IV Drips in India (Ref 3), ISRO Mars Mission (Ref 5) |
| Smart Money/Business | A company, founder, product, market, or money decision hides the real mechanism | Nokia's Collapse (Ref 4), South Korea's Rise (Ref 6) |
| Science Lite | A body, tech, nature, or everyday science topic has a simple invisible mechanism | Mosquitoes (Ref 2), Ozempic (Ref 8) |
| Sharp Contradiction | Two facts seem incompatible until the hidden logic explains them | Kerala's Literacy and Unemployment (Ref 9) |
| Viral Social Commentary | A current Indian internet debate is splitting opinion, and the mechanism beneath the disagreement is the content | The 70-Hour Work Week (Ref 15), No Kids / One Kid (Ref 16) |
| One-off | The topic is excellent but does not fit any named lane | The Voyager Golden Record (Ref 10) |

### Lane 1: Real Reason

**Use when:** the viewer thinks they already know the story, but the actual cause is different.

**Structure:** standard narrative functions in the strongest topic-native order. The pivot ("This was not personal. It was structural.") earns its weight by pointing away from the obvious reading.

**Reference calibration:** Diet Coke Shortage (Ref 7).

**Failure modes:** drifting into "the truth about" conspiracy framing; the real reason must be sourced and specific.

### Lane 2: Hidden India

**Use when:** the topic is anchored in Indian lived experience, geography, history, business, infrastructure, or behaviour — and the Indian-ness itself is the surprise.

**Structure:** standard narrative functions in the strongest topic-native order.

**Reference calibration:** Noor Inayat Khan (Ref 1), IV Drips in India (Ref 3), ISRO Mars Mission (Ref 5).

**Failure modes:** patriotic performance; pasted-on Indian relevance. A business story that happens to be Indian belongs in Smart Money, not Hidden India. The signal is: would the story still be the story if you took India out of it? If yes, route elsewhere.

### Lane 3: Smart Money/Business

**Use when:** the story is a company, founder, market, pricing decision, distribution choice, product strategy, or money mechanism.

**Structure:** perform the standard narrative functions in the strongest business-story spine. When multiple mechanisms exist, distinguish their causal roles (for example ENABLE → TRIGGER → CLOSE) instead of forcing a “First/Second” list. The mental model often takes a named form when natural.

**Reference calibration:** Nokia's Collapse (Ref 4), South Korea's Rise (Ref 6).

**Failure modes:** generic founder biography. If the Short is "company started small and became big", drop or reframe.

### Lane 4: Science Lite

**Use when:** a science, biology, health, nature, body, or tech topic can be explained one level below the surface without becoming academic.

**Structure:** standard narrative functions in the strongest topic-native order. Vocabulary control matters most in this lane: one technical term, defined inline.

**Reference calibration:** Mosquitoes (Ref 2), Ozempic (Ref 8).

**Failure modes:** too many studies, too many terms, too much explanation. One mechanism only.

### Lane 5: Sharp Contradiction

**Use when:** two facts appear to clash, and the Short resolves the contradiction with a hidden mechanism.

**Structure:** standard narrative functions in the strongest topic-native order. The turn does the heavy lifting — it breaks with the obvious reading in a single declarative sentence or through a clear causal contrast. The actual pivot in Reference 9 is: "The conventional reading is that Kerala's economy is too small to absorb its own educated youth. That is half right. It misses what happens next." Note: the phrase "Both facts are true. They explain each other." is a *resolution* line that appears in the body after the mechanism has been explained — not the pivot. Do not force it into a fixed beat slot.

**Reference calibration:** Kerala's Literacy and Unemployment (Ref 9).

**Failure modes:** picking a party-political side. Sharp Contradiction explains the *hidden mechanism* that reconciles the two facts. If the topic is a live viral debate with no hidden mechanism — only competing arguments — route to **Viral Social Commentary** instead.

### Lane 6: Viral Social Commentary

**Use when:** a current Indian internet controversy is splitting opinion. The script stages the debate, steelmans both sides, then surfaces the structural mechanism beneath the disagreement.

**Structure:** lane-specific. See `VIRAL_SOCIAL_COMMENTARY.md` for the full spec. The closing line is a one-line compressed insight, a single viewer question, and a one-line nudge to share with a named audience.

**Reference calibration:** The 70-Hour Work Week (Ref 15), No Kids / One Kid (Ref 16).

**Failure modes:** stopping at "both sides are real" without surfacing the mechanism; party-political side-taking; aphorism close; generic "what do you think?"; treating the viral object as a doorway to a timeless observation.

### Lane 7: One-off

**Use when:** the topic is excellent and clearly Anchor-Short quality, but does not fit any of the six numbered canonical lanes or any of the three proposed lanes.

**Structure:** standard narrative functions in the strongest topic-native order.

**Reference calibration:** The Voyager Golden Record (Ref 10).

**Failure modes:** using One-off to escape lane discipline. If a topic fits any other lane, use that lane. A One-off requires a written justification in the brief.

## The Three Proposed Lanes

These three lanes are calibrated by the reference scripts but are not yet formally adopted. Until they are, treat the reference script as the lane definition.

| Lane | Use when | Reference scripts |
|------|----------|-------------------|
| Forgotten Inventor | A named person built or invented something widely credited to someone else, and the credit-erasure pattern is the story | Josephine Cochrane (Ref 11) |
| Quiet Monopoly | A small set of unknown firms holds disproportionate control over something essential, and the visible market hides the structure | The ABCD Grain Cartel (Ref 12), De Beers (Ref 13) |
| Status Game | A behaviour, product, or ritual exists primarily to signal status, and the signal is what the price tracks | The Indian Wedding Budget (Ref 14) |

These three lanes share a structural cousin: each centres a named entity or system (a person, a small cartel, a public ritual) and shows how cultural memory or visible markets misrepresent it. They are not interchangeable. Forgotten Inventor centres a credit-erasure story on a named person; Quiet Monopoly centres a small set of unknown firms; Status Game centres a behaviour or product that exists primarily to signal.

If a fourth or fifth reference script gets added to any of these lanes and the channel publishes consistently in it, the lane becomes a candidate for formal adoption — at which point it earns a full entry above, alongside the seven canonical lanes.

### Lane graduation (probation)

Proposed lanes are valid to tag and script at any time but do **not** sit in the fixed rotation. The promotion / keep-testing / cut rule — and the weekly cadence it interacts with — live in `../OPERATIONS.md`. They are publishing operations, not script knowledge; no pipeline stage needs them.

## Tiebreaker — When a Topic Fits Multiple Lanes

The lane that "wins" is determined by the **hook**, not the topic.

- *"Mosquitoes do not bite randomly. They hunt specific people."* (Ref 2) → **Science Lite**. The surprise is the invisible mechanism.
- *"Britain's most decorated female spy in the Second World War was an Indian princess."* (Ref 1) → **Hidden India**. The surprise is the Indian-ness.
- *"In 2007, Nokia controlled half the global smartphone market. By 2013, it sold its phone division to Microsoft for almost nothing."* (Ref 4) → **Smart Money**. The surprise is the business mechanism.
- *"Global supply chains have a priority list. Most of the time, it is invisible."* (Ref 7) → **Real Reason**. The viewer knew the surface, not the cause.
- *"Kerala has the highest literacy rate of any Indian state. Kerala also has one of the highest unemployment rates in the country."* (Ref 9) → **Sharp Contradiction**. The surprise is two facts that resolve via a hidden mechanism.

**Hidden India tiebreaker:** Hidden India is the lane only when **Indian-ness IS the surprise**, not the setting. A business story that happens to be Indian is Smart Money. A policy quirk that exists only in India (IV drip culture, Mangalyaan, Kerala literacy) is Hidden India.

**Viral Social Commentary tiebreaker:** if the topic is a *current* Indian internet controversy where the audience is already arguing in the comments, and the Short cannot land honestly without naming both sides and surfacing the structural shift beneath the disagreement, route to Viral Social Commentary even if the topic also fits Hidden India or Sharp Contradiction. The signal is: "the debate itself is the entry point". A topic with a clean hidden mechanism and no live argument is not this lane.

If a topic genuinely cannot be assigned to one lane after applying the hook test, run it as a **One-off** with a written justification in the brief.

## Lane Declaration Format

Before scripting, the brief must declare:

```
Format lane: [Real Reason / Hidden India / Smart Money / Science Lite / Sharp Contradiction / Viral Social Commentary / One-off / Forgotten Inventor / Quiet Monopoly / Status Game]
Why this lane: [1 sentence]
Hook direction: [1–3 short spoken lines, or a one-sentence summary]
Payoff direction: [1 sentence]
Reference scripts to study: [The cited references for this lane]
External-creator clone risk check: [How this version differs from existing high-volume coverage]
```

If the lane is unclear, repair the angle before drafting. Generate a cleaner reframe until the lane is obvious, or mark it as a deliberate One-off with a written justification. Drop the topic only when no clean lane or One-off justification can be made.

## External-Creator Topic Safety Rule

If a high-volume short-form creator has already covered the same topic territory with our intended angle, our version must differ in at least three of these five areas:

1. Hook
2. Framing
3. Mechanism / mental-model angle
4. Closing line
5. Visual treatment

If it differs only by translating the same structure into another language, drop the topic.

External creators, including any YouTube channel, are never factual sources or style targets. They are market leads at most. See `SOURCE_AND_FACT_RULES.md` for the source hierarchy.

## Current Affairs Sub-Categories

Three sub-categories. The rules differ.

1. **News coverage.** Banned. Time-sensitive surface reporting that expires in a week has no place in this lane structure.
2. **Current event as doorway** (Real Reason, Hidden India, Sharp Contradiction lanes). The event is the door; the hidden mechanism is the content. Wait 2 to 5 days. The script must still make sense 6 to 12 months later.
3. **Viral debate** (Viral Social Commentary lane). The debate itself is the entry point; the structural shift beneath it is the content. The 6-to-12-month-relevance rule does not apply — these are produced for the live argument.

Maximum two current-event Shorts per week across all three categories. Never two consecutive current-event Shorts.
